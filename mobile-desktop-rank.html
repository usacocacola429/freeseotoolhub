      Mobile-First Advantages

      • Primary Ranking Signal: Google uses mobile version for indexing and ranking.
      • Higher Traffic Share: 60%+ of all searches happen on mobile devices.
      • Voice Search Ready: Mobile-optimized sites perform better in voice queries.
      • Local SEO Boost: "Near me" searches are predominantly mobile.

      Common Mobile SEO Issues

      • Slow Page Speed: Mobile networks are typically slower than desktop connections.
      • Poor Touch Targets: Buttons and links too small for finger taps (need 48px+).
      • Intrusive Interstitials: Popups that cover main content on mobile.
      • Content Disparity: Less content on mobile vs desktop version.

      Why do mobile and desktop rankings differ?

      Google uses mobile-first indexing. Differences occur due to page speed, mobile usability, and content parity between versions.

      How can I improve mobile rankings?

      Improve LCP under 2.5s, use responsive design, ensure touch targets are 48px+, and avoid intrusive popups.
