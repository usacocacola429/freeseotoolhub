Canonical Best Practices for USA/UK

  • Country-Specific Self-Referencing: .com should canonicalize to .com, .co.uk to .co.uk
  • Hreflang + Canonical Alignment: Ensure both tags agree for international pages
  • Absolute URLs with Protocol: https://www.example.com/usa-page
  • Separate Canonicals for USD/GBP Pricing Pages

International Canonical Mistakes

  • Cross-Country Canonicalization: .co.uk page canonicalizing to .com
  • Ignoring Local Parameters: ?currency=GBP not handled
  • WWW vs Non-WWW Mismatch Across Countries
  • Noindex on Local Version: Sending mixed signals

USA & UK Canonical FAQs

Should .com and .co.uk have different canonicals?

Yes, each country domain should have a self-referencing canonical pointing to its own URL.

Does this tool check for hreflang conflicts?

It validates that canonical tags don't contradict your hreflang implementation.

Can I use it for .us and .org.uk domains?

Absolutely! It works for all country-code TLDs and subdirectories.

How does Google.com vs Google.co.uk handle canonicals?

Both follow the same RFC standards, but our tool adds country-specific insights.
