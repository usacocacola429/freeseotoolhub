SSL Best Practices 2026

HTTPS is Essential: Google uses HTTPS as a ranking signal. Chrome marks non-HTTPS sites as "Not Secure".
Auto-Renewal: Enable auto-renewal via hosting or CDN. Let's Encrypt offers free 90-day certificates.
TLS 1.3 Preferred: Faster handshakes, stronger security. Remove deprecated TLS 1.0/1.1 support.
Monitor Expiry: Check monthly. Set reminders 30, 14, and 7 days before expiry.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an SSL certificate?

SSL encrypts data between website and visitors, enabling HTTPS. It's essential for security, trust, and SEO — Google uses it as a ranking signal.

How do I check SSL validity?

Enter your URL and click "Check SSL". You'll see validity status, expiry date, remaining days, issuer, TLS version, and cipher suite.

How often should I check?

Monthly. Let's Encrypt certs last 90 days; paid certs 1-2 years. Enable auto-renewal to avoid unexpected expiration.

What is TLS 1.3?

The latest protocol — faster handshakes, stronger encryption. All modern sites should support TLS 1.3 for optimal security.
