SSL Best Practices for 2026

HTTPS is a Global Standard: Google uses HTTPS as a confirmed ranking signal. All major browsers mark non-HTTPS sites as "Not Secure", impacting trust worldwide.
Auto-Renewal is Critical: Enable auto-renewal via your hosting or CDN. Let's Encrypt offers free 90-day certificates trusted globally.
Migrate to TLS 1.3: It offers faster handshakes and stronger security. Deprecated TLS 1.0/1.1 should be disabled on all servers.
Monitor Expiry Proactively: Check monthly. Set calendar reminders 30, 14, and 7 days before expiry to avoid unexpected downtime.

Frequently Asked Questions About SSL

What is an SSL certificate?

SSL (Secure Sockets Layer) encrypts data between a website and its visitors, enabling HTTPS. It's essential for security, user trust, and SEO — Google confirms it as a ranking signal.

How do I check SSL validity?

Enter any URL and click "Check SSL". You'll instantly see validity status, precise expiry date, days remaining, the issuing authority, TLS protocol version, and cipher suite.

How often should I check?

At least monthly. Let's Encrypt certificates last 90 days; paid certificates 1-2 years. Always enable auto-renewal to prevent unexpected expiration.

What is TLS 1.3?

TLS 1.3 is the latest and fastest security protocol. It provides stronger encryption and removes outdated algorithms. All modern sites should support it for optimal performance and security.
