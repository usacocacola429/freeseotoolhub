What is a QR Code?

A QR (Quick Response) code is a two-dimensional barcode that can store information like website URLs, text, contact details, and WiFi credentials. When scanned with a smartphone camera, it instantly directs users to the stored information.

Types of QR Codes You Can Generate

  • URL QR Code: Direct users to a website or landing page
  • WiFi QR Code: Share WiFi passwords without typing (popular in cafes and hotels)
  • vCard QR Code: Share contact details digitally (replaces paper business cards)
  • Text QR Code: Display any text message when scanned
  • Email QR Code: Open email app with pre-filled recipient and subject

How Businesses Use QR Codes in 2026

1. Restaurants & Cafes

Replace physical menus with QR code menus. Customers scan and view the menu on their phones. Post-pandemic, this has become standard practice in the US, UK, and Europe.

2. Retail & E-commerce

Place QR codes on product packaging to direct customers to product videos, reviews, or special offers. This is hugely popular in Amazon stores and Shopify merchants.

3. Marketing & Advertising

Add QR codes to flyers, billboards, and social media posts to track offline-to-online conversions. Marketers in Canada, Australia, and Germany use this extensively.

QR Code Best Practices for 2026

  • Test Before Printing: Always scan your QR code to verify it works
  • Size Matters: Ensure QR codes are at least 2cm x 2cm for print
  • Add a Call-to-Action: Write "Scan for Menu" or "Scan for WiFi" near the code
  • High Contrast: Use dark colors on light backgrounds for better scanning
  • Track Performance: Use UTM parameters in URLs to track scans
