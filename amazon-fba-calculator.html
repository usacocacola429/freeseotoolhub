FBA (Fulfillment by Amazon) Advantages

  • Prime Eligibility: Products automatically get Prime badge, typically increasing conversion rates by 30% or more
  • Hands-off Fulfillment: Amazon handles all storage, picking, packing, shipping, and returns management
  • 24/7 Customer Service: Amazon manages all customer inquiries, returns, and refunds
  • Buy Box Advantage: FBA sellers win the Buy Box significantly more often than MFN sellers
  • Multi-channel Fulfillment: Use FBA inventory for orders from other platforms like eBay or your website

MFN (Merchant Fulfilled Network) Advantages

  • Lower Total Fees: No FBA fulfillment or storage fees, just referral fees
  • Complete Inventory Control: You manage inventory levels and can bundle products easily
  • No Long-term Storage Penalties: Avoid fees for slow-moving inventory that sits for 365+ days
  • Better for Oversized Items: Large or heavy products are often cheaper to fulfill yourself
  • Custom Packaging Options: Add branded inserts, custom packaging, and personalized touches

Frequently Asked Questions About Amazon FBA

FBA (Fulfillment by Amazon) is a comprehensive service where you send your products to Amazon's fulfillment centers. Amazon then stores your inventory, picks, packs, and ships orders to customers, and manages all customer service and returns on your behalf. This allows sellers to focus on product sourcing and business growth while Amazon handles the complex logistics. Products fulfilled through FBA become eligible for Amazon Prime, which typically increases sales velocity by 30% or more.

Amazon FBA fees in 2026 consist of several components: Referral Fee (8-20% of selling price depending on product category), Fulfillment Fee (based on product size tier and shipping weight, ranging from $2.50-$6.85+), Monthly Storage Fee ($0.83 per cubic foot January-September, $2.40 per cubic foot October-December), and additional fees for optional services like labeling, preparation, returns processing, and long-term storage (inventory stored 365+ days). Our calculator automatically factors all these components for accurate estimates.

FBA (Fulfillment by Amazon): Amazon handles storage, shipping to customers, and customer service. Products get Prime badge. You pay fulfillment and storage fees but save time on logistics.

MFN (Merchant Fulfilled Network): You store inventory yourself and ship orders directly to customers. You pay only referral fees but must handle packaging, shipping, customer service, and returns yourself. Compare both options side-by-side with our free calculator to determine the best approach for your products.

A healthy ROI (Return on Investment) for Amazon FBA is typically 50-100%. This means for every $1 invested in product cost, you generate $1.50-$2.00 in revenue. Net profit margins of 20-30% after all Amazon fees are considered good performance. Top-performing products can achieve 30%+ margins. Use our calculator to experiment with different selling prices and find your optimal price point for maximum profitability.
