❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is Profit Margin?
Profit Margin is a financial metric that shows what percentage of sales has turned into profit. It measures how efficiently a company converts revenue into profit.
Gross Profit vs Net Profit?
Gross Profit = Revenue - COGS. Net Profit = Gross Profit - Operating Expenses - Taxes. Gross profit shows production efficiency, net profit shows overall profitability.
What is a good profit margin?
It varies by industry: Retail (10-20%), Manufacturing (20-30%), Software (70-80%), Restaurants (3-5%). Our tool provides industry benchmarks for comparison.
