What is Keyword Difficulty (KD)?

Keyword Difficulty is a score from 0 to 100 that estimates how hard it would be to rank on Google's first page for a given keyword. It's calculated by analyzing the number and quality of backlinks to the top-ranking pages. A low KD score signals a golden opportunity; a high score means you're up against giants.

Understanding Keyword Difficulty Scores: A Universal Scale

  • 0-20 (Easy): Perfect for brand new websites. Often requires minimal backlinks to rank.
  • 21-40 (Medium): Achievable for growing sites with some domain authority and a consistent content strategy.
  • 41-60 (Hard): Requires a strong, established site with a healthy backlink profile.
  • 61-100 (Very Hard): Dominated by mega-brands (Amazon, Wikipedia, etc.). Avoid unless your site has DA 70+.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is keyword difficulty the only factor to consider?

No. Always consider search intent (what the user actually wants) and search volume. A low-difficulty keyword with zero search volume isn't worth targeting.

How often should I check keyword difficulty?

Every time you plan a new piece of content. The SERPs are dynamic, and a keyword's difficulty can change as new competitors enter the space.
