Frequently Asked Questions About Amazon Product Research

Finding profitable Amazon products requires analyzing multiple factors:

  • High Demand: Look for products with 500+ monthly sales (BSR under 5000 in main categories)
  • Low Competition: Target products where top sellers have under 1000 reviews
  • Good Profit Margins: Aim for 30%+ net margin after all Amazon FBA fees
  • Price Sweet Spot: Products priced $20-$50 tend to have best profit potential
  • Year-Round Demand: Avoid highly seasonal products unless you have a strategy

Use our free Product Research Tool above to instantly analyze these metrics for any niche.

The Opportunity Score (0-100) combines multiple factors to rate product potential:

  • 80-100 (Excellent): High demand, low competition, strong margins — ideal to pursue
  • 65-79 (Good): Solid opportunity but may need differentiation strategy
  • 50-64 (Average): Requires more research — check if you can improve the product
  • Below 50 (Risky): High competition or low margins — generally avoid unless you have unique advantages

BSR (Best Sellers Rank) is Amazon's ranking system that shows how well a product sells compared to others in its category. Lower BSR = higher sales velocity.

Monthly Sales is the estimated number of units sold per month. Our tool converts BSR data into estimated monthly sales using proven algorithms.

Key Relationship:

  • BSR 1-500: ~3000-5000+ monthly sales (Top sellers)
  • BSR 500-2000: ~1000-3000 monthly sales (Strong performers)
  • BSR 2000-5000: ~400-1000 monthly sales (Good niche products)
  • BSR 5000-10000: ~150-400 monthly sales (Decent opportunity)
