What is Amazon FBA and How Does It Work Globally?

Fulfillment by Amazon (FBA) is a service where you send your products to Amazon's global network of fulfillment centers. They then store, pick, pack, ship, and provide customer service for these products. In return, you are charged specific fees. This model allows you to scale your business and offer Prime shipping to customers in the USA, UK, Canada, Australia, Japan, and across Europe.

A Complete Breakdown of Amazon FBA Fees

  • Referral Fee: A commission of 8-45% on each sale, varying significantly by product category.
  • Fulfillment Fee: Charged per unit based on the item's dimensions and shipping weight.
  • Monthly Storage Fee: A cost for storing your inventory in Amazon's warehouses, which spikes sharply during Q4.
  • Aged Inventory Surcharge: An additional fee for inventory stored for more than 271 days.
  • Closing Fee: A fixed per-item fee applied to media categories like books, DVDs, and software.
