What is an SSL Certificate?

An SSL (Secure Sockets Layer) certificate encrypts data between a user's browser and your website. It ensures that sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, and personal data remains private and secure.

⚠️ Warning: If your SSL certificate expires, your website will show a "Not Secure" warning. This can destroy trust and tank your traffic overnight. Always monitor your SSL expiry date!

Why SSL Matters for SEO

  • Google Ranking Boost: HTTPS is a confirmed ranking factor since 2014
  • Trust & Credibility: Users trust sites with the padlock icon (🔒)
  • Browser Compatibility: Modern browsers block or warn on HTTP pages
  • Payment Compliance: PCI-DSS requires SSL for any site accepting payments

Types of SSL Certificates

  • Domain Validated (DV): Basic encryption, ideal for blogs and small sites
  • Organization Validated (OV): Verifies organization identity, good for businesses
  • Extended Validation (EV): Highest trust level, shows company name in address bar
  • Wildcard SSL: Covers main domain and all subdomains
