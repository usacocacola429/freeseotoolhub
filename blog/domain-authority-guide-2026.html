What Exactly is Domain Authority (DA)?

Domain Authority is scored on a 100-point logarithmic scale. It's not a Google ranking factor, but it's the industry's best comparative metric to gauge your website's SEO strength versus competitors. Key components include:

  • Linking Root Domains: The number of unique websites linking to you
  • Total Backlinks: Overall link profile quality and quantity
  • MozRank & MozTrust: Link equity and trustworthiness

Frequently Asked Questions About Domain Authority

What is a "good" Domain Authority score?

Generally, 40-50 is average, 50-60 is good, and 60+ is excellent. However, DA is relative — always compare against your direct competitors.

How often is DA updated?

Moz updates its DA index every 3-4 weeks. Our tool reflects the latest available scores.

Can a new website have a high DA?

A brand new domain starts at DA 1. Building DA takes 3-6 months of consistent SEO work.
