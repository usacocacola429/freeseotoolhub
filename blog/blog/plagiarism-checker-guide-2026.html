What is Plagiarism?

Plagiarism is the act of using someone else's words, ideas, or work without giving proper credit. It can be intentional or accidental. In the academic world, plagiarism can result in:

  • Failing grades or course expulsion
  • Degree revocation
  • Legal consequences for copyright infringement
  • Damage to professional reputation

Types of Plagiarism to Watch For

  • Direct Plagiarism: Copying text word-for-word without quotation marks
  • Self-Plagiarism: Reusing your own previously published work
  • Mosaic Plagiarism: Changing a few words but keeping the same structure
  • Accidental Plagiarism: Forgetting to cite sources or misquoting
