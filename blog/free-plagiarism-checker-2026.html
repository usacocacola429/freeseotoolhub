What is Plagiarism? A Universal Definition

Plagiarism is the act of presenting someone else's words, ideas, or intellectual property as your own without proper acknowledgment. It's a serious ethical breach that can have severe consequences worldwide, including:

  • Academic Penalties: Failing grades, suspension, or even expulsion from institutions.
  • Legal Repercussions: Copyright infringement lawsuits and financial penalties.
  • Professional Devastation: Permanent damage to your credibility and career prospects.
  • SEO De-indexing: Google may remove plagiarized pages from its search results entirely.

The 4 Main Types of Plagiarism to Avoid

  • Direct Plagiarism: Copying text word-for-word without quotation marks or a citation.
  • Self-Plagiarism: Reusing significant portions of your own previously published work without disclosure.
  • Mosaic (or Patchwork) Plagiarism: Borrowing phrases and sentence structures while changing a few words here and there.
  • Accidental Plagiarism: Unintentionally failing to cite a source or misattributing a quote.

4 Proactive Tips to Ensure 100% Original Work

  1. Master the Art of Paraphrasing: Read, understand, and then rewrite the concept entirely in your own words and unique style. Never just swap synonyms.
  2. Cite Everything Diligently: Any idea, data, or quote that isn't your own needs a proper, correctly formatted citation.
  3. Use Quotation Marks for Direct Lifts: If you must use a sentence verbatim, always enclose it in quotes and provide an in-text citation.
