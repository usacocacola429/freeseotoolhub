What is a QR Code and How Does It Work?

A QR (Quick Response) code is a sophisticated, two-dimensional barcode that can store a significant amount of data. When a user points their smartphone camera at it, the code instantly directs them to a URL, displays text, connects them to WiFi, or saves a contact—creating a seamless, touchless interaction.

Types of QR Codes You Can Generate for Free

  • URL QR Code: Instantly send customers to your website, a specific landing page, a promotional offer, or a social media profile.
  • WiFi QR Code: A global hospitality standard. Let guests connect to your network by scanning, without ever asking for or typing a password.
  • vCard QR Code: The modern, dynamic replacement for paper business cards. Anyone who scans it can instantly save your full contact details to their phone.
  • Text QR Code: Display any custom message, instructions, or a special code when scanned.
  • Email QR Code: Pre-populate an email with the recipient address and subject line, perfect for customer support or B2B interactions.

5 Professional Best Practices for QR Codes in 2026

  • Always, Always Test: Scan your generated code with multiple devices (iPhone & Android) before printing thousands of copies.
  • Mind the Size & Zone: For print, ensure a minimum size of 2cm x 2cm. Keep a clear, unprinted "quiet zone" around the code.
  • Frame It with a Compelling CTA: Don't just leave a bare code. A simple "Scan for Free WiFi" or "Scan to See the Menu" triples the scan rate.
  • Ensure High Contrast: A dark QR code on a light background is non-negotiable for fast, reliable scanning.
  • Track Your Marketing ROI: Use UTM-tagged URLs to measure exactly how much traffic, engagement, and revenue your QR code generates in Google Analytics.
