What is a Masked Aadhaar? A Lesson in Secure PII Design

In an official Masked Aadhaar, the first 8 digits of the 12-digit number are replaced with "XXXX-XXXX," displaying only the last 4 digits (e.g., XXXX-XXXX-1234). This allows for identity verification without exposing the full, exploitable personal number. This simple, powerful concept of redaction is directly applicable to any national ID, Social Security Number (SSN), passport number, or tax ID anywhere in the world.

📊 A Global Privacy Lesson (2026 Data): The Identity Theft Resource Center reports that over 10,000 fraud cases in India in 2025 were linked to the exposure of full Aadhaar numbers. This is a global trend. Whether it's an SSN in the US or a NINO in the UK, casual sharing of full ID numbers is the primary vector for financial identity theft.

Universal Scenarios Where You Should Mask a Personal ID

  • Hotel Check-ins Globally: From Paris to Phuket, hotels often request an ID copy. Provide a masked copy to prevent misuse.
  • Online Freelancing & Gig Platforms: When a client or platform in Berlin or Boston asks for "ID proof," a masked version fulfills the requirement without the risk.
  • Travel & Mobility: For train, flight, or bus bookings where a supporting ID is needed but full details are not legally mandatory for the ticket.
  • Third-Party Service Verification: When activating a SIM card, signing a rental agreement, or verifying an account with a new digital service.
